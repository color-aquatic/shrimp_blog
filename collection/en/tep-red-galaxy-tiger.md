# Red Galaxy Tiger Shrimp

## Introduction

Red Galaxy Tiger Shrimp is a perfect combination of vibrant red coloration and tiger stripes along with sparkling galaxy spots. This is one of the most beloved cold-water shrimp species thanks to its dynamic and distinctive beauty.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Caridina cf. cantonensis "Red Galaxy Tiger"
- **Size**: 2.5-3 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Bright red with black stripes and white galaxy spots
- **Temperament**: Active, enjoys exploring
- **Care difficulty**: Difficult (requires cold-water shrimp experience)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 20-24°C |
| pH | 5.8-6.8 |
| TDS | 100-160 ppm |
| GH | 3-6 dGH |
| KH | 0-2 dKH |

## Biological Features

Red Galaxy Tiger originates from breeding between Tiger and Galaxy lineages. The tiger pattern creates dynamic vitality while galaxy spots add mystery and attraction.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: Minimum 50 liters
2. **Filter**: Strong biological filtration system
3. **Heater**: Heater with thermostat
4. **Aquatic plants**: Moss, Bucephalandra

### Diet

- Premium cold-water shrimp food
- High-protein foods: Bloodworms, spirulina
- Natural foods: Catappa leaves

## Breeding

More difficult to breed than Neocaridina. Females carry eggs for 25-30 days with 15-25 shrimplets.

## Lifespan

2-2.5 years under good conditions.

## Notes

🐅 **Distinctive**: Tiger patterns make them outstanding focal points in aquascaping tanks.

⚡ **Active**: Compared to other cold-water shrimp, Red Galaxy Tiger is quite dynamic and easy to observe.
