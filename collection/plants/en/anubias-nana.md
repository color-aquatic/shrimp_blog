# Anubias Nana

## Introduction

Anubias Nana (Anubias nana) is one of the most popular and easy-to-grow aquatic plants. With dark green, glossy leaves and excellent adaptability, it's an ideal choice for beginner aquascapers.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific Name**: Anubias nana
- **Origin**: West Africa (Cameroon) 
- **Size**: 5-15 cm in height
- **Appearance**: Round, thick, dark green glossy leaves
- **Growth Rate**: Very slow

## Growing Conditions

| Parameter | Required Range |
|-----------|----------------|
| Temperature | 22-28°C |
| pH | 6.0-8.0 |
| Water Hardness | Soft to hard |
| Lighting | Low to medium |
| CO2 | Not required |

## Growth Characteristics

Anubias Nana grows from a creeping rhizome and produces thick leaves with distinct petioles. The plant is long-lived and tolerates a wide range of water conditions, making it ideal for aquascaping.

## Planting and Care

### Planting:
- **IMPORTANT**: Never bury the rhizome in substrate
- Tie rhizome to wood or rocks with fishing line
- Can use specialized aquarium glue
- Let roots naturally attach to surfaces over time

### Care:
- Requires low light, suitable for low-tech aquariums
- Liquid fertilization through water column is effective
- Clean leaves regularly to prevent algae buildup
- Remove old, yellow leaves to stimulate new growth

### Benefits:
- Extremely easy to grow, perfect for beginners
- No CO2 or strong lighting required
- Provides shelter for small fish and shrimp
- Helps prevent algae development

## Aquascaping Applications

Anubias Nana is widely used for:
- Foreground plant in small aquariums
- Midground plant in large aquariums
- Attached to driftwood for natural scenes
- Green accent in simple aquascapes

## Popular Varieties

- **Anubias nana "Petite"**: Smaller size (3-8 cm)
- **Anubias nana "Golden"**: Yellow-green leaves
- **Anubias nana "Snow White"**: Leaves with white variegation

## Propagation

The plant reproduces by:
- Dividing rhizome when mature
- Each rhizome section must have at least 3-4 leaves
- New plants will develop from these rhizome sections

## Important Notes

- Very slow growth (1-2 new leaves per month)
- Prone to algae coverage under strong light
- May flower under suitable conditions
- Can survive emersed with adequate humidity
- Never bury the rhizome to prevent rot
- Great for low-maintenance setups
