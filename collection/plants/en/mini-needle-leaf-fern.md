# Mini Needle Leaf Fern

## Introduction

Mini Needle Leaf Fern (Microsorum pteropus "Needle Leaf") is a special variant of Java Fern with narrow, needle-like leaves. This plant is loved in aquascaping for its unique appearance and good adaptability.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific Name**: Microsorum pteropus "Needle Leaf"
- **Origin**: Southeast Asia (cultivated variant)
- **Size**: 10-20 cm in height
- **Appearance**: Narrow leaves, 15-25 cm long, 2-5 mm wide
- **Growth Rate**: Slow

## Growing Conditions

| Parameter | Required Range |
|-----------|----------------|
| Temperature | 18-30°C |
| pH | 5.5-8.0 |
| Water Hardness | Soft to hard |
| Lighting | Low to medium |
| CO2 | Not required |

## Growth Characteristics

Mini Needle Leaf Fern has a creeping rhizome and produces distinctively needle-shaped leaves. The leaves are dark green and may curve slightly, creating dynamic effects in water flow.

## Planting and Care

### Planting:
- **IMPORTANT**: Never bury the rhizome in substrate
- Tie rhizome to wood or rocks
- Can be left free-floating in aquarium
- Prefers shaded locations

### Care:
- Requires low light, suitable for low-tech tanks
- Liquid fertilization through leaves is effective
- Trim old leaves to stimulate growth
- Avoid frequent repositioning

### Benefits:
- Easy to grow with minimal technical requirements
- Creates unique visual effects
- Effective nitrate absorption
- Ideal spawning site for fish

## Aquascaping Applications

Mini Needle Leaf Fern is commonly used for:
- Midground or background plant
- Accent plant in low-tech aquariums
- Attached to driftwood for forest scenes
- Combined with other fern species

## Propagation

The plant reproduces through:
- Dividing rhizome when mature
- Plantlets growing from old leaves
- Spores on leaf undersides

## Important Notes

- Very slow growth requires patience
- Leaves break easily if handled roughly
- Cannot tolerate strong lighting
- Excellent choice for low-light aquariums
- Can grow emersed with high humidity
- Perfect for beginner aquascapers
