# African Fern

## Introduction

African Fern (Bolbitis heudelotii) is a beautiful and unique aquatic fern, popular in aquascaping for its distinctive leaf shape and easy care. It originates from Africa and grows well in aquatic conditions.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific Name**: Bolbitis heudelotii
- **Origin**: West and Central Africa  
- **Size**: 15-40 cm in height
- **Appearance**: Compound leaves, deeply divided
- **Growth Rate**: Slow to medium

## Growing Conditions

| Parameter | Required Range |
|-----------|----------------|
| Temperature | 20-28°C |
| pH | 5.0-7.5 |
| Water Hardness | Soft to medium |
| Lighting | Medium |
| CO2 | Recommended |

## Growth Characteristics

African Fern grows from a rhizome and produces compound leaves with finely divided segments. The plant attaches firmly to rough surfaces and can develop into large clumps over time.

## Planting and Care

### Planting:
- **IMPORTANT**: Never bury the rhizome in substrate
- Tie rhizome to wood or rocks with fishing line or glue
- Keep rhizome in direct contact with water
- Choose location with gentle water flow

### Care:
- Provide medium light, avoid strong lighting
- Regular liquid fertilization
- Remove old, dead leaves to stimulate new growth
- Create gentle water movement around the plant

### Benefits:
- Creates unique focal points in aquascaping
- Natural shelter for fish and shrimp
- Absorbs excess nutrients from water
- Prevents erosion at planting sites

## Aquascaping Applications

African Fern is commonly used for:
- Midground plant in natural layouts
- Attached to driftwood for forest scenes
- Combined with rocks for waterfall effects
- Accent plant in African biotope tanks

## Important Notes

- Never bury the rhizome in substrate
- Prefers gentle water flow
- Slow growing — patience is needed
- May compete with algae under strong light
- New leaves are reddish-brown, mature leaves are dark green
- Sensitive to sudden parameter changes
