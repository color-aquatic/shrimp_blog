# Pelia Moss

## Introduction

Pelia Moss (Monosolenium tenerum) is a unique aquatic liverwort with a distinctive leaf-like appearance, highly valued in aquascaping. This plant has a slow growth rate but creates stunning visual effects in aquarium layouts.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific Name**: Monosolenium tenerum  
- **Origin**: East Asia
- **Size**: 2-5 cm in height
- **Appearance**: Flat, thick thalli, dark green color
- **Growth Rate**: Slow

## Growing Conditions

| Parameter | Required Range |
|-----------|----------------|
| Temperature | 18-26°C |
| pH | 5.5-7.5 |
| Water Hardness | Soft to medium |
| Lighting | Medium to high |
| CO2 | Not required but recommended |

## Growth Characteristics

Pelia moss develops in flat, thick lobes forming dense clusters. Unlike other mosses, Pelia doesn't have a main stem but spreads horizontally, creating distinctive green carpets.

## Planting and Care

### Planting:
- Can be attached to wood or rocks with fishing line
- Can be left free-floating in the aquarium
- Avoid burying as it may cause rot

### Care:
- Regular trimming to maintain shape
- Remove dead parts to prevent contamination
- Provide adequate light and CO2
- Maintain stable water parameters

### Benefits:
- Provides hiding places for shrimp and fry
- Absorbs nitrates, improving water quality
- Enhances aquarium aesthetics
- Releases oxygen, improving water environment

## Aquascaping Applications

Pelia moss is commonly used for:
- Foreground plant in Dutch layouts
- Accent plant in Nature Aquarium
- Combined with rocks and driftwood for natural landscapes
- Creating unique textures in aquascapes

## Important Notes

- Avoid frequent repositioning
- Requires time to adapt to new environments
- Slow development requires patience
- Sensitive to sudden environmental changes
