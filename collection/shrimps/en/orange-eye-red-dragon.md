# Orange Eye Red Dragon Shrimp

## Introduction

Orange Eye Red Dragon Shrimp is a premium tiger shrimp line recognized for its bright orange eyes and intense red dragon-like body pattern. Its high contrast look and relative rarity make it highly desirable among collectors.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Caridina sp. "Orange Eye Red Dragon"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Deep red tones with distinct orange eyes
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, active in groups
- **Care difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced (stable water required)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 22-26°C |
| pH | 5.2-6.6 |
| TDS | 120-180 ppm |
| GH | 4-6 dGH |
| KH | 0-2 dKH |

## Biological Features

Orange Eye Red Dragon is commonly categorized among high-grade tiger shrimp, valued for clear orange eyes and strong red pigmentation. Color intensity depends heavily on stable water parameters, substrate quality, and feeding.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: Minimum 30 liters, shrimp-dedicated setup preferred
2. **Substrate**: Active substrate (ADA/Benibachi) for stable low pH
3. **Filter**: Gentle biological filtration, avoid excessive flow
4. **LED lighting**: Moderate intensity
5. **Aquatic plants**: Moss and fine-leaf plants for shelter and grazing

### Diet

- High-quality Caridina shrimp pellets
- Blanched vegetables: Spinach, zucchini, carrots
- Natural biofilm and soft algae
- Mineral supplements for healthy molting

## Breeding

Females usually carry eggs for about 25-30 days, and brood size is typically moderate. To maintain strong phenotype consistency, select high-quality breeders and keep water parameters stable.

## Special Notes

- Avoid large single water changes
- Do not keep with active fish species
- Feed controlled portions to prevent organic overload

## Lifespan

1.5-2 years under optimal care.
