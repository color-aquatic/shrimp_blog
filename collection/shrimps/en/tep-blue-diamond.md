# Blue Diamond Shrimp

## Introduction

Blue Diamond Shrimp is an eye-catching Neocaridina variety with a distinctive blue coloration that shimmers under light. Easy to keep, easy to breed, and a beautiful addition to any planted tank — great for both beginners and experienced hobbyists.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Blue Diamond"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Diamond blue, shimmering under light
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, active in small groups
- **Care difficulty**: Easy (suitable for beginners)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 20-26°C |
| pH | 6.5-7.8 |
| TDS | 150-300 ppm |
| GH | 4-8 dGH |
| KH | 1-5 dKH |

## Biological Features

Blue Diamond Shrimp belongs to the Neocaridina family, adapting well to various water conditions. Their distinctive blue color comes from light reflecting off the shell, producing an attractive shimmer.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: Minimum 20 liters, can be kept with other shrimp species
2. **Filter**: Gentle biological filtration system
3. **LED lighting**: Moderate lighting to enhance coloration
4. **Aquatic plants**: Moss, ferns for hiding spots

### Diet

- Specialized shrimp pellets
- Blanched vegetables: Carrots, pumpkin, spinach
- Natural foods: Biofilm, soft algae

## Breeding

Blue Diamond Shrimp breed easily in stable conditions. Females carry eggs for about 20-30 days and produce around 20-40 shrimplets per batch.

## Lifespan

1-2 years under proper care conditions.

## Notes

💡 **Pro tip**: To enhance coloration, feed shrimp carotenoid-rich foods and maintain stable water conditions.
