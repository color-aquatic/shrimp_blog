# Snow Ball Shrimp

## Introduction

Snow Ball Shrimp is an aquarium shrimp species with pristine snow-white coloration, creating a pure and gentle beauty in aquascaping tanks. With their unique color and charming character, they make excellent focal points for planted tanks.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Snow Ball"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Pure white like snow
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, actively swimming
- **Care difficulty**: Easy (suitable for beginners)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 18-26°C |
| pH | 6.0-7.8 |
| TDS | 150-350 ppm |
| GH | 4-12 dGH |
| KH | 1-6 dKH |

## Biological Features

Snow Ball is a white color variant of the Neocaridina line created through selective breeding. The pristine white color makes them stand out beautifully against green plants and creates stunning visual effects.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: 15 liters and above
2. **Filter**: Gentle filtration, avoid strong currents
3. **Lighting**: Moderate lighting to enhance white color
4. **Substrate**: Dark or green substrate for contrast

### Diet

- Specialized shrimp food
- Plant matter: Seaweed, soft algae
- Vegetables: Carrots, blanched cabbage

## Breeding

Snow Ball Shrimp breed easily. Females carry eggs for about 3-4 weeks with 20-40 shrimplets per batch.

## Lifespan

1-2 years under proper care conditions.

## Notes

❄️ **Pro tip**: Keep on dark substrate to highlight their pristine white coloration.
