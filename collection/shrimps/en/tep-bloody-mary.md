# Bloody Mary Shrimp

## Introduction

Bloody Mary Shrimp is a striking ornamental shrimp with a deep red body and a nearly transparent head. The name comes from its rich blood-red coloration, which creates a captivating look in planted tanks.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Bloody Mary"
- **Size**: 2-3 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Deep dark red with transparent head
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, prefers group activity
- **Care difficulty**: Intermediate (requires basic experience)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 20-24°C |
| pH | 6.2-7.2 |
| TDS | 150-250 ppm |
| GH | 4-8 dGH |
| KH | 1-4 dKH |

## Biological Features

Bloody Mary Shrimp is selectively bred from the Cherry shrimp line. The standout feature is a deep red body while the head and swimming legs are nearly transparent, creating a unique contrast.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: Minimum 30 liters to maintain stable environment
2. **Filter**: Biological filtration system with gentle flow
3. **Heater**: Maintain stable temperature
4. **Aquatic plants**: Cryptocoryne, Anubias to create natural environment

### Diet

- Premium shrimp food
- High-protein foods: Spirulina, dried bloodworms
- Fresh vegetables: Pumpkin, blanched carrots

## Breeding

Under stable conditions, females carry eggs for 20-25 days and produce 15-30 shrimplets. Keeping the water parameters steady helps improve survival rates among the young.

## Lifespan

1.5-2 years with proper care.

## Notes

🔥 **Note**: Bloody Mary needs good nutrition to maintain their characteristic deep red color.
