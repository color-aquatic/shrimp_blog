# Thai Yellow Shrimp

## Introduction

Thai Yellow Shrimp is an ornamental line with a vivid yellow-orange color that stands out in any planted tank. Hardy, easy to breed, and especially beginner-friendly.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Yellow"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Yellow-orange, from light to deep shades
- **Temperament**: Peaceful and active
- **Care difficulty**: Easy (beginner friendly)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 22-31°C |
| pH | 6.0-7.8 |
| TDS | 150-300 ppm |
| GH | 7-9 dGH |
| KH | 2-6 dKH |

## Biological Features

Thai Yellow Shrimp adapts well to community planted setups. Under stable conditions, this line breeds consistently, helping you build a healthy colony quickly.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: 30cm or larger
2. **Filter**: Gentle biological filtration, avoid strong flow
3. **Lighting**: Moderate intensity
4. **Substrate**: Dark substrate to enhance yellow coloration
5. **Aquatic plants**: Moss and fine-leaf plants for shelter

### Diet

- Quality ornamental shrimp pellets
- Natural biofilm and soft algae
- Blanched vegetables: Zucchini, carrots, leafy greens

## Breeding

Females usually carry eggs for around 3-4 weeks and produce consistent batches of shrimplets. During introduction, slow acclimation for about 10 minutes is recommended to reduce stress.

## Lifespan

Around 1-2 years under proper care conditions.

## Notes

Perform regular partial water changes and keep parameters stable to maintain vibrant color and steady breeding.
