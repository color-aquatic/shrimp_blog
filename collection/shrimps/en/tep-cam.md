# Orange Shrimp

## Introduction

Orange Shrimp is a popular and easy-to-keep aquarium shrimp species with vibrant orange coloration. With excellent adaptability and affordable pricing, it's a perfect choice for beginners or anyone looking to add colorful accents to their aquascaping tanks.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Orange"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Vibrant orange from light to deep shades
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, active both day and night
- **Care difficulty**: Very easy (ideal for beginners)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 18-25°C |
| pH | 6.0-8.0 |
| TDS | 150-350 ppm |
| GH | 3-15 dGH |
| KH | 1-8 dKH |

## Biological Features

Orange Shrimp originated from selective breeding of red Cherry shrimp. They have excellent adaptability to various water conditions and are not picky eaters, making care extremely easy.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: 10 liters and above
2. **Filter**: Simple sponge or hang-on filter
3. **Heater**: Only needed during cold winter months
4. **Aquatic plants**: Java moss, Anubias

### Diet

- Commercial shrimp food
- Green vegetables: Water spinach, blanched cabbage
- Natural foods: Algae, biofilm

## Breeding

Orange Shrimp breed prolifically. Females can carry eggs every 3-4 weeks with 20-50 offspring per batch.

## Lifespan

15-18 months under normal conditions.

## Notes

⚠️ **Important**: Avoid mixing Orange Shrimp with other colored shrimp species to prevent crossbreeding and color loss in future generations.
