# Black Galaxy BOA Shrimp

## Introduction

Black Galaxy BOA Shrimp is one of the most premium cold-water shrimp lines available, prized for its BOA (Black on All) pattern — a deep, solid black body accented with bright galaxy-like spots. This is an exceptionally high-value shrimp suited only for experienced keepers.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Caridina cf. cantonensis "Black Galaxy BOA"
- **Size**: 2.5-3.2 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Pure black with BOA pattern and galaxy spots
- **Temperament**: Very shy, rarely active during daytime
- **Care difficulty**: Very difficult (requires years of experience)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Required Value |
|-----------|----------------|
| Temperature | 18-22°C |
| pH | 5.5-6.5 |
| TDS | 80-120 ppm |
| GH | 2-5 dGH |
| KH | 0-1 dKH |

## Biological Features

Black Galaxy BOA represents the peak of selective shrimp breeding. The BOA (Black on All) pattern means the entire body is covered in deep black coloration, combined with bright spots that create a striking night-sky effect.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: Minimum 80 liters, professional setup
2. **Filter**: Premium 3-stage filtration system
3. **Heater**: Titanium heater with precise thermostat
4. **Substrate**: ADA Amazonia or equivalent

### Special Diet

- Premium Japanese shrimp food
- Natural foods: Oak leaves, dried banana leaves
- Enzyme and probiotic supplements

## Breeding

Very difficult to breed. Only about 5-10 shrimplets per batch, with a survival rate below 30%.

## Lifespan

3-4 years in ideal conditions.

## Special Notes

🏆 **Class**: One of the rarest shrimp in the hobby, suited only for serious and dedicated keepers.

⚠️ **Important**: You should have several years of cold-water shrimp experience before attempting this species.

💎 **Collection value**: Each shrimp carries significant value, making them a prized addition to any collection.
