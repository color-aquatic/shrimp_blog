# Extreme Red / Wine Red Shrimp

## Introduction

Extreme Red / Wine Red Shrimp is a beautiful ornamental variety with a distinctive wine-red and white coloration. This eye-catching look makes Wine Red highly sought after, though the species needs patience and experience to keep well.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Caridina sp. "Extreme Red / Wine Red"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Wine red and white pattern resembling red wine
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, active in groups
- **Care difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced (can be finicky)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature |20-26°C |
| pH | 5 - 6.5 |
| TDS | 90-120 ppm |
| GH | 4-6 dGH |
| KH | 0-2 dKH |

## Biological Features

Extreme Red / Wine Red belongs to the Taiwan Bee family and needs soft, acidic water to thrive. The wine-red pigmentation develops best under stable conditions with proper nutrition. These shrimp are fairly sensitive to water parameter changes.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: Minimum 30cm tank for optimal breeding conditions
2. **Substrate**: ADA Amazonia Gold or Benibachi recommended for pH buffering
3. **Filter**: Gentle sponge filtration to preserve beneficial bacteria
4. **LED lighting**: Moderate intensity to enhance red coloration
5. **Aquatic plants**: Java moss, Christmas moss for grazing and hiding

### Diet

- High-quality bee shrimp pellets
- Blanched vegetables: Spinach, carrots, zucchini
- Natural biofilm and micro-algae
- Color-enhancing supplements with astaxanthin

## Breeding

Extreme Red / Wine Red breeding requires stable water parameters and patience. Females carry eggs for 25-28 days and produce 20-30 shrimplets per batch. Maintaining the wine-red color through generations requires careful breeder selection.

## Special Notes

- **Commercial size**: Sold at 0.8-1.2cm (gender cannot be determined at this size)
- **Water stability**: Critical for health and color development
- **Temperature sensitivity**: Avoid rapid temperature changes

## Lifespan

1.5-2 years with proper care and stable conditions.
