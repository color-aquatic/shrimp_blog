# Fire Red Shrimp

## Introduction

Fire Red Shrimp is a highly popular ornamental shrimp line known for its deep, solid red coloration. With strong adaptability and hardy behavior, Fire Red is suitable for both beginners and experienced hobbyists who want a bold color accent in planted tanks.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Fire Red"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Deep red with strong body coverage
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, active both day and night
- **Care difficulty**: Very easy (ideal for beginners)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 18-25°C |
| pH | 6.0-8.0 |
| TDS | 150-350 ppm |
| GH | 3-15 dGH |
| KH | 1-8 dKH |

## Biological Features

Fire Red is developed through selective breeding from red Cherry shrimp to improve color depth and body coverage. This line is hardy, omnivorous, and adaptable to many tank setups.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: 10 liters and above
2. **Filter**: Simple sponge or hang-on filter
3. **Heater**: Only needed during cold winter months
4. **Aquatic plants**: Java moss, Anubias

### Diet

- Commercial shrimp food
- Green vegetables: Water spinach, blanched cabbage
- Natural foods: Algae, biofilm

## Breeding

Fire Red Shrimp breeds readily in stable conditions. Females can carry eggs every 3-4 weeks with 20-50 offspring per batch.

## Lifespan

15-18 months under normal conditions.

## Notes

⚠️ **Important**: Avoid mixing Fire Red with other colored Neocaridina lines if you want to preserve pure red coloration over generations.
