# Fire Red Shrimp

## Introduction

Fire Red Shrimp is a hugely popular ornamental shrimp known for its deep, solid red coloration. Hardy and adaptable, it's great for both beginners wanting an easy first shrimp and experienced hobbyists looking for a bold color accent in their tanks.

## Characteristics

- **Scientific name**: Neocaridina davidi "Fire Red"
- **Size**: 2-2.5 cm when fully grown
- **Coloration**: Deep red with strong body coverage
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, active both day and night
- **Care difficulty**: Very easy (ideal for beginners)

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 18-25°C |
| pH | 6.0-8.0 |
| TDS | 150-350 ppm |
| GH | 3-15 dGH |
| KH | 1-8 dKH |

## Biological Features

Fire Red is selectively bred from red Cherry shrimp to improve color depth and body coverage. This line is hardy, omnivorous, and adapts easily to many tank setups.

## Care Instructions

### Required Equipment

1. **Tank**: 10 liters and above
2. **Filter**: Simple sponge or hang-on filter
3. **Heater**: Only needed during cold winter months
4. **Aquatic plants**: Java moss, Anubias

### Diet

- Commercial shrimp food
- Green vegetables: Water spinach, blanched cabbage
- Natural foods: Algae, biofilm

## Breeding

Fire Red Shrimp breed readily in stable conditions. Females can carry eggs every 3-4 weeks, producing 20-50 shrimplets per batch.

## Lifespan

15-18 months under normal conditions.

## Notes

⚠️ **Note**: Avoid mixing Fire Red with other colored Neocaridina lines if you want to keep the red color pure over generations.
