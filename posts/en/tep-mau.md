# Color Shrimp (Neocaridina)

Color shrimp of the genus Neocaridina are the most popular and beloved aquarium shrimp today. With their diverse colors, gentle nature, and ease of care, they are an ideal choice for both beginners and experienced hobbyists.

## Overview of Color Shrimp (Neocaridina)

Color shrimp have the scientific name *Neocaridina davidi* (formerly *Neocaridina heteropoda*). They originate from Taiwan and have been selectively bred to create the beautiful color variants we see today.

### Characteristics

- **Size**: 2-3 cm when fully grown
- **Lifespan**: 1-2 years
- **Colors**: Diverse (red, yellow, blue, orange, black, white, blue...)
- **Temperament**: Peaceful, harmless to fish and other shrimp species
- **Breeding**: Easy, breeds in captivity
- **Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy (suitable for beginners)

### Common Types of Color Shrimp

1. **Red Cherry Shrimp** (Red Cherry) - Bright red, most popular
2. **Yellow Thai Shrimp** (Yellow Cherry) - Bright yellow
3. **Green Jade Shrimp** (Green Jade) - Emerald green
4. **Orange Shrimp** (Orange Sakura) - Deep orange
5. **Chocolate Shrimp** (Black Rose) - Glossy black
6. **Blue Dream Shrimp** (Blue Dream) - Beautiful blue
7. **Snowball Shrimp** (Snowball) - Pure white
8. **Rili Shrimp** - Colored head and tail, transparent middle

## Care Conditions

### Water Parameters

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | 18-28°C (optimal: 22-26°C) |
| pH | 6.5-8.0 (optimal: 7.0-7.5) |
| Hardness (GH) | 4-15 dGH |
| Carbonate Hardness (KH) | 2-8 dKH |
| Ammonia (NH3) | 0 ppm |
| Nitrite (NO2) | 0 ppm |
| Nitrate (NO3) | < 20 ppm |

### Tank Size

- **Minimum**: 20 liters for a small group (10-15 shrimp)
- **Recommended**: 40-60 liters for good growth space
- **Larger**: The bigger, the better, helps stabilize water environment

## Tank Setup

### Required Equipment

1. **Filter**: 
   - Hang-on-back or external filter
   - Avoid filters with strong suction
   - Can use sponge filter - safe for baby shrimp

2. **Heater**:
   - If room temperature < 20°C
   - Choose appropriate wattage for tank volume

3. **Lighting**:
   - LED lights to save electricity
   - Lighting time: 6-8 hours/day
   - Helps aquatic plants grow

4. **Aeration**:
   - Small air pump to increase dissolved oxygen
   - Especially important when keeping high density

### Substrate and Decorations

- **Substrate**: 
  - Fine sand or small gravel
  - Avoid substrates with sharp edges
  - Can use specialized shrimp substrate

- **Aquatic Plants**:
  - Java Moss, Weeping Moss
  - Java Fern
  - Easy-growing plants
  - Provides hiding places and food sources for shrimp

- **Driftwood and Rocks**:
  - Creates hiding places for shrimp
  - Helps beneficial bacteria grow

## Feeding

Color shrimp (Neocaridina) are omnivorous and not picky eaters. They can eat:

### Natural Foods

- **Algae**: Natural algae in the tank
- **Biofilm**: Bacterial film on surfaces
- **Leaves**: Mulberry leaves, dried Indian almond leaves (soaked in water)

### Commercial Foods

- **Specialized shrimp pellets**: Contains complete nutrition
- **Spirulina algae**: Helps enhance colors
- **Frozen foods**: Artemia, bloodworms (feed sparingly)

### Feeding Schedule

- **Frequency**: 1-2 times/day
- **Amount**: Just enough for shrimp to finish in 2-3 hours
- **Note**: Don't overfeed, avoid polluting the water

## Care and Maintenance

### Water Changes

- **Frequency**: 20-30% per week
- **New water**: Must be dechlorinated and at room temperature
- **Avoid**: Changing too much water or too suddenly

### Water Quality Testing

- Use test kit for regular testing
- Pay special attention to NH3, NO2, NO3
- Adjust pH and hardness if needed

### Tank Cleaning

- Siphon substrate periodically
- Clean filter according to instructions
- Avoid over-cleaning (removes beneficial bacteria)

## Breeding

Color shrimp (Neocaridina) breed very easily in good conditions.

### Pregnancy Signs

- Females have "saddle" (yellow/green area on back) before laying eggs
- Swollen belly with visible eggs inside
- Gestation period: 3-4 weeks

### Caring for Baby Shrimp

- Baby shrimp are very small, need safe hiding places
- Food: Natural algae, crushed food
- Avoid filters with strong suction
- High survival rate if environment is stable

## Common Problems

### Sudden Death

**Possible causes**:
- Poor water quality (high NH3, NO2)
- Changing too much water or too suddenly
- Sudden temperature changes
- Lack of oxygen

**Solutions**:
- Test water quality immediately
- Change water gradually
- Ensure adequate aeration

### Color Fading

**Causes**:
- Stress
- Poor water quality
- Lack of nutrition
- Genetics (some naturally have lighter colors)
- Keeping different colors together (will interbreed and lose pure color)

**Solutions**:
- Improve water quality
- Feed foods rich in carotenoids (spirulina algae)
- Select colorful individuals for breeding
- **Important**: Only keep one color in one tank to maintain pure color

### Not Breeding

**Causes**:
- Lack of males or females
- Unsuitable environment
- Stress

**Solutions**:
- Ensure both males and females (ratio 1:2 or 1:3)
- Check and adjust water parameters
- Create a quiet environment with many hiding places

## Notes on Keeping Multiple Colors

⚠️ **Important**: If you want to maintain pure color of each type, **DO NOT keep different colors together** in the same tank. When kept together, they will interbreed and offspring will lose color, reverting to brown/wild type.

**Solutions**:
- Keep each color in a separate tank
- Or accept interbreeding if you want to create new colors

## Conclusion

Color shrimp (Neocaridina) are excellent aquarium shrimp for both beginners and experienced hobbyists. With diverse colors, easy care, and good breeding, they will create a colorful and lively aquarium.

> **Advice**: Be patient and observe your shrimp regularly. Each tank has its own characteristics, and you will learn a lot through the care process. If you want to maintain beautiful colors, keep one color in one tank!
